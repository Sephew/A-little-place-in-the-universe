# Universe Tree — Design Brief

*A journal that witnesses you, and a tree that proves you were here.*

---

## The one-sentence version

You write into the dark. Something answers, gently. And a tree — yours, unlike anyone else's — grows from what you gave it.

---

## Why this exists

Most journaling apps are filing cabinets. You put things in, and the things sit there. Nothing changes, nothing notices, nothing accumulates into anything you can *look at*.

Most habit apps are the opposite problem: they notice too aggressively. They count, they streak, they guilt. They turn showing up into a score, and the moment you break the chain they punish you for it — which is precisely when you needed them most.

This is trying to sit between those. Something that receives what you give it, reflects it back, and turns it into visible, permanent, beautiful evidence that you existed and paid attention. Not a score. A *thing that grew*.

---

## The two pillars

### A. Someone witnesses you

Not a coach. Not a therapist. Not an advisor. A **witness**.

The distinction matters and it's the whole product. A coach tells you what to do next. A therapist digs. A witness says: *I saw that. I noticed you've said something like it before. That was hard and you did it anyway.*

**What the witness does:**
- Reflects back a quote to ponder on, that might help you so you can hear it from outside your own head... not always, sometimes its good enough for it to acknowledge what you're doing is valid.
- Notices patterns across time — the thing you keep circling, the fear that shows up in three different disguises, the win you didn't register as a win
- Marks the passage of things: *you were dreading this two weeks ago and now you're on the other side of it*
- Speaks rarely and briefly. Silence is a feature.

**What the witness never does:**
- Give advice unless explicitly asked
- Cheerlead ("You've got this!!") or perform enthusiasm
- Diagnose, label, or reach for clinical vocabulary
- Ask a follow-up question just to keep engagement up
- Pretend the bad thing was secretly good

The tone to aim for: someone who has known you a long time, is not alarmed by you, and doesn't need you to be okay right now.

### B. You grow your own universe tree

Every entry feeds a tree. The tree is generated, luminous, singular, and yours — no one else's looks like it, and yours will never look like anyone else's.

It is the app's central claim, made visible: *you are unrepeatable.*

The tree lives in a dark, vast, quiet space. It is the only light source. This is not incidental — the whole visual thesis is one small unique thing glowing in an indifferent universe, and it's *still worth looking at*.

Everyone starts of first with the same sapling, after 7 days of input, it will grow into a tree depending on what you "feed" it as input.

---

## The core design rule (do not break this)

> **The tree grows because you showed up, not because you were happy.**

A vent grows the tree exactly as much as a win does. A confession of failure grows it as much as a triumph.

If the tree wilts when you're struggling, you've built something that abandons you precisely when you need it. You'd open the app on a bad day, see a dying tree, feel worse, and close it. The feedback loop would eat itself.

So: **content never diminishes the tree. Content only changes its character.**

A hard month might cool the light, thicken the trunk, draw mist across the ground, deepen the color. Different — not lesser. Struggle should visibly become part of the tree's beauty, because *that is the actual thesis of the app.* A tree that has only ever known good days should look thinner and less interesting than one that's been through something.

---

## What the tree encodes

Two categories, and keeping them separate is what makes the whole thing work.

**Permanent (fixed the moment you begin — never changes):**
- Species and silhouette — the fundamental shape it will always have
- Branching character — the angle, the taper, the way it curls or reaches
- Base palette — the color it glows - (Choice between "let the universe decide for me" and "I want to choose this")

This is your fingerprint. It is decided before you write a single word, and it never moves. It exists so the tree feels like a *fact about you*, not a report card.

**Living (responds to how you use the app):**
- Size and density — grows with every entry, full stop
- Weather and light — responds to the emotional texture of recent entries
- Ground, mist, particles, distant stars — the world the tree stands in

## Anti-goals

Things this must never become, listed so you can catch yourself:

- **Not a streak app.** No chains to break. No "you lost your 40-day streak." Coming back after three months away should feel like a homecoming, not a walk of shame.
- **Not gamified.** No points, no levels, no badges, no XP. The tree is not a reward, it's a *record*.
- **Not social.** No shared trees, no comparison, no feed. The uniqueness is meaningful precisely because it isn't competing with anyone.
- **Not a productivity tool.** Nothing here should make you more efficient.
- **Not a nag.** It may notice you haven't written. It may never guilt you about it.
- **Not clinical.** No mood scores, no 1–10 sliders, no charts of your sadness over time. Reducing a feeling to a number is the exact thing this app is a rebellion against.

## The loop
0. **You log in the Website*, you are greeted by the landing page, account registration then move on to the main hub itself.
1. **You arrive.** Dark space, your tree, silence. No dashboard, no prompts, no unread badge.
2. **You give it something.** A voice in the universe prompts you "Welcome back (User), tell us your story" A win. A failure. A struggle. Or you just vent into the void with no shape at all — all four are equally valid inputs and none is treated as better than another.
3. **The tree takes it.** Immediately and visibly. A branch, a shift in the light, a new drift of particles. The response should be *felt*, not announced, with a hum, a tone, or a soft song.
4. **Something witnesses.** Sometimes right away. Sometimes not until the end of the week. It should not be predictable — a witness that responds on schedule is a machine, and you'll feel it. It appreciates your stories, and wants you to know you are seen. Ensure that some "event" will play. If the witness does not talk, make the witness narrate something like, a meteor said hello, you are lucky. or The universe seems to be so loud yet it makes no noise..."
5. **You leave.** No streak counter, no "come back tomorrow", no notification scheduled.

---

## Success criteria

Ranked, because they'll conflict:

1. **You open it on a bad day.** This is the only test that really matters. If it's only usable when you're already doing well, it has failed at its entire purpose.
2. **You look at the tree without writing anything.** That means it became a thing worth having, not just a byproduct of a chore.
3. **You're still using it in six months.** Not daily. Just — still.
4. **You'd be sad if you lost the tree.** That's when it stopped being a visualization and started being *yours*.

**Explicitly not a success criterion:** daily active use. An app you open when you actually need it, twelve times a year, that helps every one of those twelve times, is a better app than one you open every day out of streak anxiety.

---

## The feeling to protect

Look up at the night sky and you feel small. That's the easy half.

The harder, better feeling — the one this app is chasing — is looking up, feeling small, and then looking at the one impossible glowing thing that only exists because you kept showing up, and understanding that *both are true at once*. You are insignificant and you are the only one of you. The universe is indifferent and you made a light in it anyway.

Every design decision should be checked against that. If a feature makes the app feel like a productivity tool, a game, or a therapist, it's wrong — no matter how much it would boost retention.

## Open questions

- **How much should the witness say, and how often?** Too much and it's a chatbot. Too little and it's a diary with extra steps. This probably has to be found by using it, not by deciding it.
- **Can you re-read old entries?** Yes. by pressing the "witness" you can select different options such as settings & view old entries
- **What happens after years?** A tree can't grow forever without becoming a shapeless mass. Does it mature and stop? Yes.
After 31 days the tree matures, the witness will ask you if you want to seed another tree. You can revisit the old tree and the entries you have made.
- **What does the very first tree look like, before you've written anything?** An animation plays planting the seed, then a sapling bursts open. For the first 7 days its only the sapling, then a tree will sprout out (a tree that grows with entries & expands its branches)
